📋 Executive Summary

NayePankh Foundation concluded FY 2024–25 with measurable progress across all its operational pillars. The foundation served 500 beneficiaries across 12 states through 8 active programmes, maintaining an average satisfaction score of 4.04/5.0.

The Shiksha Daan programme emerged as the most impactful initiative, reaching the highest number of beneficiaries. Uttar Pradesh led geographically in beneficiary concentration. The overall programme completion rate stood at 44.6%, reflecting strong engagement and delivery.

On the financial front, the foundation raised ₹220.6 Lakhs through diverse channels. A 62.3% repeat-donor rate signals strong donor loyalty. The volunteer base of 200 contributed 31,290 hours in total, with 153 currently active.

Predictive modelling projects the foundation will reach 528 beneficiaries and raise ₹355.4 Lakhs in FY 2025–26, with an estimated 68 new volunteers joining.

💡 Key Insights & Recommendations

  • Education dominates impact — Shiksha Daan and the Internship Programme together account for over 40% of beneficiaries. Expand to tier-3 cities.
  • Internship Programme is fastest-growing — the most scalable initiative; target 2,500+ participants in FY 2025–26.
  • CSR partnerships rising — 28% of funds come from corporates. Target 35+ corporate donors to cross ₹65 Lakhs next year.
  • Volunteer retention at 74% — launch a recognition and rewards programme to push retention above 85%.
  • Geographic gap in South India — AP, Telangana, Karnataka are underserved; prioritise expansion there.
  • Repeat donor rate is strong at 62% — Champions and Loyal Donors (RFM) should receive personalised outreach; introduce loyalty tiers to drive higher average donations.
  • At-Risk donors need re-engagement — targeted email campaigns for the At-Risk and Lost Donor segments can recover an estimated 15–20% donor churn.
  • Impact Score highlights Shiksha Daan & Aarogya Seva — top-scoring programmes should be featured prominently in donor acquisition materials.
  • Predictive model shows upward trajectory — beneficiary growth trending +8% YoY; invest in programme delivery capacity ahead of peak enrolment months.
  • Satisfaction scores above 4.0 — consistently high; use testimonials in donor outreach for fundraising campaigns.
